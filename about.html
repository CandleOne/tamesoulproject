Preamble

The African and African American populations of the world have long been mistreated, oppressed, and demonized. This alienation and general hatred of our people have led to an — in some cases intentional — divide between our population and the broader global community.

This separation has materialized in a discrepancy in regards to the educational, economic, and emotional values within our communities. The primary objectives of the Tame Souls Project aim to move the Black community towards a more sustainable, forward-looking, and stable way of life, and to push the broader world forward in regards to acceptance and the development of a more inclusive society.

What does it mean to tame the soul?

The African soul is an inherently fiery, energetic entity. This energy is ancestral and is derivative of the spirit that our predecessors required to survive the harsh conditions of our mother continent, and has been strengthened by the conditions that our people have been subjected to throughout modern history.

The souls of the Black population have been vilified, suppressed, and distorted, to the benefit of those who stand to gain from the oppression of the Black population. To tame the soul is not to kill that fire our souls possess, but rather to redirect and channel that energy in a way that is beneficial to oneself and one's community.

How the Black soul is used against itself

Systemic forces have long turned the strengths of the Black soul into weapons against us. Understanding these mechanisms is the first step toward reclaiming our narrative.

Black confidence painted as boisterousness — our self-assurance is reframed as arrogance or aggression to undermine our authority and presence.
Reactiveness to mistreatment portrayed as unbridled anger or aggression — our justified responses to injustice are pathologized rather than understood.
Glorification of criminality and violence — through private prison profiteering and media manipulation, harmful narratives are incentivized while education, morality, and emotional regulation are minimized.
Physical traits and characteristics — athleticism, skin tone, and hair — are portrayed as animalistic, undesirable, or threatening, rather than celebrated as expressions of beauty and ancestral heritage.
Cultural traditions depicted as uncivilized while being co-opted as popular culture for the economic gain of those who have suppressed their originators.
Over-sexualization and masculinization of our youth — adulthood is forced upon Black children, stripping them of innocence and burdening them with expectations no child should carry.

"The energy our ancestors needed to survive — that same fire — is ours to redirect. Not extinguish. Not suppress. Redirect."
